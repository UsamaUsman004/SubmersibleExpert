Sumbersible Pumps

Submersible pumps are operated by hermetically sealed motors and are, as their name suggests, used to pump liquid that they are fully submerged in. The pump operates by pushing the liquid it is submerged in up to the surface.

Like in a centrifugal pump, the pump consists primarily of a casing and impeller. The sealed motor drives the impeller to draw water up into the pump, where the force of the spinning impeller against the casing sends the water around and out of the pump and up a pipe or hose to the surface.

Submersible pumps help avoid issues with pump cavitation, where the pump begins to form bubbles of vapor and inhibit correct pump function. Being completely submerged helps prevent this issue from occurring and enables better continued use without interruption.
